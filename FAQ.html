Frequently Asked Questions

Pedal Shaale, organised by Karnataka Non-Motorised Transport Authority in association with Citizens for Sustainability, Bangalore Bicycling School and others, is a Certified Bicycle Training Program that intends to facilitate interested citizens to learn cycling and equip them with necessary skills to confidently navigate in Indian traffic on a cycle. Free cycling training is provided to enhance the uptake of cycling as a sustainable mode of transport in Bengaluru for work, education, leisure, short errands etc.

  • Organisations interested in conducting bicycle training programmes enrol in the program. They should have employees/staff who are willing to become trainers.
  • The trainer is expected to sign up to the program by filling the trainers form. (Organisation can fill on behalf of the trainer as well). The trainer must undergo a minimum of 6 hours of training and qualify the test under instructors from Bangalore Bicycling School.
  • The organisation along with the trainer finds training location near the organisation as per the guidelines. The location verification must be done by the organisations and then by the project coordinator. The necessary permissions must be obtained from the local authority / property owner / equivalent authority.
  • The organisation can start promoting Pedal Shaale. The organisation must ensure that the customer is well informed about the Pedal Shaale Program.
  • Interested individuals who wish to learn cycling can enrol into the Pedal Shaale Program by filling out the centralised participants sign up form.
  • The participant will be provided free training by the trainers for a minimum of 10 hours followed by theoretical and practical tests. The participant must qualify the test to receive the certification.
  • For quality check and verification purposes the participant is required to fill the feedback form.

    • Any established organisation like Bicycle stores, non-profit organisation, Resident Welfare Associations, any other registered organisation interested in training cyclists at no cost can become a Pedal Shaale training partner organisation.

  • To identify suitable staff from their organisation to attend training conducted by Instructors till they are certified to be fit as trainers to impart training for the participants. The minimum duration of the training is six hours.
  • To ensure the availability and management of the certified trainers to facilitate smooth conducting of the training program as the guidelines.
  • To identify appropriate and safe space for conducting the bicycling training.
  • To arrange cycles in good condition for the training.

  • At any point of the training period, no amount of money is to be charged from the participants.
  • The organisation must ensure that the trainer is behaving appropriately with the participants, especially with women.
  • The organisation providing the training will take full accountability for actions of the trainer.
  • The branding guidelines of Pedal Shaale will be followed for promoting and executing the program.

  • People willing impart training to the participants as per the program guidelines though theoretical and practical sessions are Pedal Shaale trainers.
  • The organisation for which he or she is working has to first sign up as a partner for the Pedal Shaale program after which the trainer has to fill the trainer sign up form.
  • After which the trainer / organisation coordinator will be contacted and the further details on the training location, timings etc, will be shared.
  • Note: A trainer has to be associated with an organisation, individual entries will be considered invalid.
  • After which he/she has to undergo training of minimum 10 hours which includes theoretical and practical training.
  • After the completion of the training, a test will be conducted by the Pedal Shaale Instructors passing which the trainer qualifies to become a Certified Pedal Shaale trainer.

  • Educate their customers about the Pedal Shaale Program.
  • Enrol their customers in the program by filling up the signup form provided by the stakeholders on behalf of the participants or guide them while filling the form.
  • Train the participant as per guidelines the trainer will learn during the training sessions with the instructors.
  • Take feedback from the participants after the completion of the training in the form provided.
  • Trainers must also keep records of the participants' details and the progress they made during the course period. They must record the data in the specified forms provided for the purpose.
  • The trainer must follow all his roles and responsibilities which is mentioned above, failing which his/her eligibility as a Trainer will be terminated.

  • Organisers shall select areas in their choice of locations as per the below guidelines.
  • Low or No traffic area/road. Example: playground, parking lot, driveway of an institution etc.
  • Even surface. Example: paved, tarred, stoneless ground etc.
  • Road width. Enough space for the cycle learner to drift sideways without hitting any obstacle.
  • Gentle slope of 1-3 degrees. Too much slope is dangerous because the cycle learner will go too fast.

    Citizens who are interested to undergo bicycle training to prepare themselves for navigating safely and confidently on Indian city roads. He/She is expected to fill the Participants sign up form either online or at the store.

  • Women participants can bring one member of their family or a close friend with them to the session.
  • Children must be more than 12 years old and be accompanied by their parents during the session.

  • The Participants must coordinate time with the trainer / organisation and start undertaking the training.
  • The participants must be patient, understanding and learn with interest.
  • The participant is expected to behave appropriately with the trainer.
  • The participant must undergo a simple online theoretical test and practical test conducted by the trainer to qualify the Pedal Shaale Program.
  • The participant must fill the feedback form for further improvements in the program.

    • The participants manual is a guidebook to know the rules of the road for cyclists. It will teach them how to behave on the roads, road safety rules and signages, and how to distinguish between various types of infrastructure. This will be provided to the participants during the course.

    About

    This is an initiative of Karnataka Non-Motorised Transport Agency (KNMTA) in association with Citizens for Sustainability (CiFoS), Bangalore Bicycleing School (BBS) and partner organisations. The target is to train as many people as possible in Karnataka to learn to cycle confidently on Indian streets.
