Frequently Asked Questions

Pedal Shaale is a Certified Bicycle Training Program that intends to facilitate interested citizens to learn cycling and equip them with necessary skills to confidently navigate in Indian traffic on a cycle. Free cycling training is provided to enhance the uptake of cycling as a sustainable mode of transport in Bengaluru for work, education, leisure, short errands etc.

This is organised by the Directorate of Urban Land Transport (DULT) in association with Citizens for Sustainability (CiFoS), Bangalore Bicycling School (BBS), Karnataka Bicycle Dealers Association (KBDA), and other partners that may evince interest in the program.

  • Instructors: They are experienced bicycle trainers who will first evaluate the trainers and train the trainers through a well-defined process. Based on the certification of the instructors, a candidate applied for the role of trainer gets qualified. The instructors will be responsible to train the trainers.
  • Trainers: They will impart training to the participants as per the program guidelines through theoretical and practical sessions.
  • Participants: Citizens who are interested to undergo bicycle training to prepare themselves for navigating safely and confidently on Indian city roads. The prospective trainees include those who do not know how to ride a bicycle and people who know cycling but lack confidence to ride within city traffic.

  • Organizations interested in conducting bicycle training programs enrol in the program by filling up the organization sign up form. Based on the evaluation of the application, organizations will be onboarded for the Pedal Shaale program
  • Once the organization is onboarded, they are required to identify suitable staff from its organization to attend training conducted by Instructors till they are certified to be fit as trainers to impart training for the participants.
  • The onboarded organization needs to ensure that the trainers sign up form is filled by the identified trainers or by the organization on behalf of the trainers. The list of identified trainers must be provided to the Program Coordinator/DULT.
    The trainer must undergo a minimum of six (6) hours of training (theoretical and practical) and qualify in the test conducted by the instructors as per the prescribed format. Upon completion of the training, the instructor will evaluate the skill sets acquired by the trainer and fill a trainer’s graduation form. On the basis of the evaluation, the instructor will certify him/her fit to become a trainer. The feedback of the trainers will be gathered to understand the efficacy of the train the trainer program and to incorporate valuable suggestions, if any.
  • In conjunction with the above, the organization (preferably along with their trainers) will identify an appropriate and safe training location near the organization. The identified location has to be verified and approved by the Project Coordinator before the finalization of the training location. Necessary permissions must be obtained from the local/appropriate authority/property owner, if required. The relevant details of how to choose an appropriate and safe training locations are as follows.
    • Low or no traffic area/low traffic road. Example: playground, parking lot, driveway of an institution etc. However, it should not be an isolated location which participants may find unsafe to come and get trained.
    • Even surface such as paved, tarred, stoneless ground etc. The slope should be gentle (1-3 degrees).
    • Road width - Enough space should be available for the cycle learner to drift sideways without hitting any obstacle.
    • Adequate lighting has to be ensured if the trainings are planned early morning or evenings.
  • Once all approvals are in place, the organization can publicise about the training programs through different platforms to ensure that potential candidates are well informed about the program. During the outreach programs, the organizations are expected to follow the prescribed terms and conditions relating to branding etc.
  • Interested individuals who wish to learn cycling can enrol into the Pedal Shaale Program by filling out the participant’s signup form, or the trainers/organization will help the participants to fill the form. The organization has to ensure all assistance for conduct of training under this program by facilitating necessary support such as arranging assets including bicycles in good condition for training.
  • The participant will be provided free training by the trainers for a minimum of 10 hours followed by theoretical and practical tests. Upon the successful completion of the training, the trainer will evaluate the skill sets acquired by the participants and fill a participant’s graduation form. The participants who successfully complete the training will receive a certificate.
  • The experience of the participants on the training program will be captured through a participant’s feedback form to help in evaluating and improving the program. This will be done by the respective trainers.

    • Any established organization like bicycle stores/dealers, bicycle manufacturers, non-profit organization, registered Resident Welfare Associations (RWAs), any other registered organizations / trusts / societies interested in training cyclists at no cost can apply for Pedal Shaale training as partner organization.

    The terms and conditions of the program are given in the organization signup form (as referred to in FAQ 4)

    Interested individuals who wish to learn cycling through the Pedal Shaale Program are requested to go through FAQs related to the program and to fill the participant’s signup form. The form facilitates participants to opt for one of the available locations based on their convenience [closer to their home/school/work etc.]. The individuals can fill up the form either by themselves or with the help of the trainers/organizations. The form also provides the option to choose their preferred time for the training and to specify the language they find most comfortable using while speaking with their trainer.
    As mentioned earlier, free training will be provided for the participants (by the trainers) for a minimum of 10 hours, and it will be followed by theoretical and practical tests. Once enrolled for the program, the participants are expected to:
    • To attend all the classes as per the schedule.
    • To follow the instructions given by the Trainer and go through the reference materials provided during the training.
    • To handle the assets used with utmost care during the training sessions.
    • To undergo theoretical and practical tests.
    • To fill the feedback form after completion of the training course and provide genuine feedback on the course.

    The participant’s manual is a guidebook to know the rules of the road for cyclists. It will teach them how to behave on the roads, road safety rules to be followed, understanding relevant signages, how to distinguish between various types of infrastructure etc. This will be provided to the participants during the course.

    About

    This is an initiative of Karnataka Non-Motorised Transport Agency (KNMTA) in association with Citizens for Sustainability (CiFoS), Bangalore Bicycleing School (BBS) and partner organisations. The target is to train as many people as possible in Karnataka to learn to cycle confidently on Indian streets.
